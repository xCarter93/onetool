## Design Context

### Users
Small field-service business owners and their teams (cleaning, landscaping, HVAC, trades). They're often on-the-go, not desk-bound. They need to manage clients, send quotes, track invoices, and schedule tasks without wrestling with software. Many are coming from spreadsheets, paper, or clunky legacy tools. They want to look professional to their clients while spending minimal time in the app itself.

### Brand Personality
**Friendly, Modern, Approachable.** OneTool should feel like a smart, helpful teammate — not a corporate tool or a toy. The tone is warm but competent, casual but never sloppy. Think "the friend who's great with spreadsheets" rather than "enterprise software vendor."

### Emotional Goals
The interface should make users feel:
- **Ease & relief** — "This is so much easier than what I was doing before"
- **Speed & efficiency** — "I get things done fast here, no friction"
- **Confidence & control** — "I've got my business handled"
- **Professionalism & pride** — "My quotes and invoices look legit"

### Aesthetic Direction

**Workspace UI**: Calm, functional, and polished. Inspired by **Stripe Dashboard** (clean data presentation, trustworthy, professional) and **Notion** (flexible, content-first, warm). The workspace should feel soft and quiet — generous whitespace, subtle borders, gentle shadows. Let the user's data be the visual focus, not the chrome.

**Landing Page**: Can be bolder and more expressive. The current neobrutalism aesthetic (offset shadows, bold borders, parallax) works for marketing energy but should NOT bleed into the workspace.

**Theme**: Light and dark mode, defaulting to system preference. Both modes should feel equally considered — dark mode is not an afterthought.

### References & Anti-References

**Aspire to**: Stripe Dashboard (data clarity, trustworthiness), Notion (warmth, flexibility, content-first)

**Explicitly avoid**:
- Generic SaaS templates — cookie-cutter dashboards with stock illustrations and bland card grids
- Enterprise complexity — Salesforce-style overwhelming density and feature overload
- Bare/brutalist in the workspace — too stripped down feels unfinished or cheap

### Design System Foundations

- **Font**: Outfit — clean, modern, geometric sans-serif with friendly character
- **Colors**: OKLch color space. Primary cyan-blue (`rgb(0, 166, 244)`). Semantic colors for success (emerald), warning (amber), danger (red)
- **Components**: Base primitives (Radix UI + react-aria) with styled variant layer using `tailwind-variants`
- **Icons**: Lucide React (primary), Heroicons and IntentUI icons (supplementary)
- **Animations**: Framer Motion for UI transitions, GSAP for complex sequences, Lenis for smooth scroll. Animations should be purposeful and subtle in the workspace — never decorative noise
- **Border radius**: 0.5rem base (rounded-lg), scaling from 0.25rem to 1.5rem
- **Spacing**: Consistent 4px grid. Component padding typically 16-24px. Generous whitespace between sections

### Design Principles

1. **Content over chrome** — The user's data (clients, invoices, tasks) is the star. UI elements should recede and let information breathe. Avoid decorative elements that compete for attention.

2. **Obvious over clever** — Every interaction should be immediately clear. No hidden gestures, mystery icons, or ambiguous states. A trades business owner checking invoices between jobs should never have to think about how the UI works.

3. **Fast by feel** — The interface should feel snappy and responsive. Use optimistic updates, smooth transitions, and minimal loading states. Perceived performance matters as much as actual performance.

4. **Professional output** — Client-facing materials (quotes, invoices, emails) should look polished enough that users feel proud sharing them. The app's professionalism reflects on their business.

5. **Calm density** — Show useful information without overwhelm. Not too sparse (wastes screen real estate), not too dense (creates anxiety). Find the sweet spot where a glance tells you what you need.

### Accessibility

- Target: **WCAG 2.1 AA** compliance
- Ensure sufficient color contrast in both light and dark modes
- Support keyboard navigation throughout
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for users who need it
- Screen reader compatible with proper ARIA attributes (already using Radix/react-aria foundations)
